[00:00:00] **Speaker 1:** It's about care. Care is the only ingredient you can't fake. You can't mandate it with a checklist. You can't value-engineer it into existence. You can't find it copying an old facade. Care is the decision to treat the ordinary objects in our lives as if they matter, because the people who use them do. You don't always see care. Sometimes care looks like a hand-carved stone sculpture. Other times it looks like a boring handrail that quietly guides you down a subway station staircase. If you take the time to slow down and look for it, you can feel the human spirit inside these objects that surround us. And I think that's what all of this arguing is really about. When people say the world is ugly, half the time what they mean is, "The world feels indifferent, like nobody's steering, like nothing is loved." We're surrounded by decisions made by people who will never have to live with them. I think what we all really want is a world where more things are made with the quiet message, "You matter enough for me to try." So, if you take one thing from this video, let it be that. Don't romanticize the past. Don't demonize the present. Just demand care. These videos take hundreds of hours to make. I read a thousand pages of articles and books. I talk to industry experts, and I reflect on my 15 years of experience in the design industry. I care very deeply about the things that I discuss here.
