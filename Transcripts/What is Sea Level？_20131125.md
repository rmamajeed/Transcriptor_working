## Discussion Topics

- **Here are 3-5 key ideas from the transcription:** 
- **Complexity of Defining Sea Level**:** The common understanding of sea level is challenged by the Earth's non-spherical shape and the need to define a reference point for landlocked areas or under mountains where no ocean exists.
- **Earth's Irregular Shape and Gravitational Field**:** The Earth's spinning motion creates an equatorial bulge, and its uneven internal density along with the mass of continents cause local variations in gravity, which significantly influence the actual level and distribution of the oceans.
- **The "Mountain Conundrum"**:** The very presence and gravitational pull of large landmasses like mountains and continents affect the surrounding sea level, drawing water towards them and complicating how a universal "sea level" reference can be established for heights.
- **Geodesy and the Earth Gravitational Model**:** Geodetic scientists define sea level based on the Earth's complex gravitational field, utilizing the "Earth Gravitational Model" to accurately predict ocean levels globally and to establish a consistent reference for heights, even beneath landmasses.
